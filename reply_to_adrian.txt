Adrian — love the LEV thread. Your post pushed me toward a small constructive idea instead of just asking ChatGPT whether immortality is likely.

I'm cautious about treating any single LLM as a judge for something the size of LEV. But we *can* ask the same question to many models through OpenRouter and treat the result as a benchmark of how LLMs currently frame longevity claims — not as scientific proof, just as a consistency mirror for the movement.

I ran three snapshots today.

(1) Fifty of the cheapest text models on OpenRouter, one sample each (~$0.01). Of 45 that responded, only 7 leaned "likely / high" within this century; 30 framed it as "possible" or "unclear", and 8 leaned "low" or "near zero".

(2) Twenty-seven recent flagship models — GPT-5.x, Claude 4.5/4.6/4.7 Opus/Sonnet/Haiku, Gemini 3.x, Grok 4.x, DeepSeek v4, Qwen3-Max, Mistral Large/Medium, one sample each (~$0.16). 27/27 responded. The bucket split was almost even: 13 leaned "low / unclear / near-zero", 13 leaned "possible / high". Same lab landed in different buckets across model versions.

(3) Eight mid-tier flagship models, **ten independent samples each at temperature 0.7** to measure *within-model* variance (~$0.34). 76/80 succeeded. This is the most interesting one: the Anthropic models swing between "near-zero" and "possible" across resamples of the same prompt; Mistral Large swings from "near-zero" to "high"; Google Gemini 3.1 Pro parks in "unclear"; Grok scatters across the whole spectrum. Within-model spread is real and uneven by lab.

The headline isn't "LEV is likely" or "LEV is implausible." It's that today's frontier models disagree *across each other* and even *with themselves* on resampling — which is the cleanest case I can make for not using any single AI as an oracle for a 30-year longevity timeline.
